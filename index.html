Prisoner's Dilemma

The Prisoner's Dilemma is a famous problem in game theory that shows how two individuals might not cooperate, even if it seems in their best interest to do so.

The Story

Two people are arrested for a crime. They are questioned separately and cannot communicate. They have two choices: Cooperate (stay silent) or Defect (betray the other).

  • If both cooperate: each gets 2 years in prison.
  • If one defects while the other cooperates: the defector goes free, and the cooperator gets 5 years.
  • If both defect: each gets 4 years.
